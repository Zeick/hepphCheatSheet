\footnotetext{
For left-handed fermions, the superfield will be written as, e.g., $Q$, and it contains a scalar component $\sqL$ and a chiral fermion $Q_\alpha$.
Their complex conjugates will be shown as $\sqL^*$ and $\bQ_\dalpha$ as is done in the previous section.

For right-handed fermions such as $\bU$, the superfield will be written as $\bU$. Its scalar component is written as (or, in other words, equivalent to) $\suR^*$, and its fermionic one is $\Uc_\alpha$. {\em c is just a label;} does not mean charge or complex conjugation.
Their complex conjugates are $\suR$ and $\bUc_\dalpha$.

They form a Dirac fermion as $U=\pmat {u_\alpha\\\bUc{}^\dalpha} =: \pmat{U\s L\\U\s R}$; its {\em charge} conjugate is $U\suprm C=\pmat {U\suprm C_\alpha\\\bar u^\dalpha}$.

Majorana fermions are written as, e.g., 
$\bno=\bno\suprm C=\pmat{\bno_\alpha\\\bbno{}^{\dalpha}}$. Here $\bbno_{\dalpha}$ is the complex conjugate of $\bno_\alpha$.
